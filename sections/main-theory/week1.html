Introduction

Welcome to the Small Box unit! In the first week you will be introduced to safe workshop practices and the fundamentals of reading working drawings, selecting timber and marking out your components. Accuracy and care at this stage make the rest of the project much easier.

Measuring and marking

Accurate measuring and marking are essential when working with timber. Use a ruler, square and marking knife or pencil to transfer dimensions from the drawing to your workpiece. Measure twice, mark once and always check that your marks are clear and precise before cutting.

Cutting components

After marking out, cut the sides and base pieces to length using a backsaw, mitre box or other appropriate saw. Secure the timber in a vice or with clamps before cutting and keep your fingers away from the blade. Wear your safety glasses and hearing protection, and work at a steady pace to maintain control.
