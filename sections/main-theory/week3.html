Sanding and surface preparation

Surface preparation is key to a professional finish. Start sanding with a coarse grit to remove tool marks, then progress to finer grits to create an even surface. Always sand with the grain and wipe away dust between grits. Pay attention to edges and corners to avoid rounding them over unless that is part of your design.

Applying a finish

Once the surface is smooth and clean, apply a finish such as a natural oil or wax. Use a cloth to spread the finish evenly, allow it to penetrate the wood and wipe off any excess. Multiple thin coats often provide a better result than one thick coat. Let the finish cure fully before handling the box.

Evaluation and reflection

Finally, evaluate your completed box. Does it function as intended? Are the joints tight and square? Does the divider slide smoothly? Consider the appearance and how well your finish protects the timber. Think about the sustainability of the materials and processes you used and note what you would do differently next time.
