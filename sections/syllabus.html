The small box project addresses a range of outcomes from the Stage 5 Industrial Technology syllabus. As you progress through the unit you will:

  • IND5‑1 – Work safely: Identify, assess and manage risks when using tools, equipment and materials. Follow Work Health and Safety procedures at all times.
  • IND5‑2 – Apply design principles: Read and interpret working drawings, measure and mark accurately, and apply design principles to produce a functional box.
  • IND5‑3 – Use tools and processes: Select and use hand and machine tools to cut, assemble and finish your project with care and precision.
  • IND5‑4 – Select materials: Consider timber properties and sustainability when selecting materials and adhesives.
  • IND5‑6 – Collaborate effectively: Participate in collaborative work practices such as peer feedback, helping classmates and sharing tools safely.
  • IND5‑7 – Transfer skills: Apply the measuring, cutting and finishing skills learnt in this unit to other contexts and projects.
  • IND5‑8 – Evaluate products: Evaluate your finished box in terms of function, aesthetics, construction quality, economics and environmental impact.
  • IND5‑9 – Explore technologies: Describe and analyse both traditional and contemporary timber construction techniques and tools.
  • IND5‑10 – Understand impacts: Evaluate how timber technologies impact society, the environment and culture locally and globally.

As you complete the theory and practical activities, consider which outcomes you are addressing and record your progress in your folio.
