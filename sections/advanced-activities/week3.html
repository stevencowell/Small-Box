Advanced Week 3: Finishing & Evaluation

Finishing not only protects the wood but also enhances its beauty. Natural oils penetrate the timber and bring out the grain, while waxes provide a soft sheen and tactile feel. Lacquers and varnishes create a hard, protective surface that resists wear and moisture, but they may alter the colour of the timber and contain solvents. Shellac offers a traditional finish with a warm glow but is less durable in wet conditions. Consider the environmental impact of each product — many modern oils and waxes are made from renewable resources.

Self‑evaluation is an important part of craftsmanship. By reflecting on both strengths and weaknesses you can plan how to improve your skills and processes in future projects.

Advanced Activities – Week 3 Tasks

  1. Compare oil, wax and lacquer finishes for wooden boxes. Discuss their appearance, durability and environmental impact.

  2. Evaluate your completed box: identify two strengths of your work and two areas you could improve next time.
